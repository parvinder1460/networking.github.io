What is Vlan (Virtual LAN)

VLAN is a custom network which is created from one or more local area networks. It enables a group of devices available in multiple networks to be combined into one logical network. The result becomes a virtual LAN that is administered like a physical LAN. The full form of VLAN is defined as Virtual Local Area Network. How VLAN works

  1. VLANs in networking are identified by a number.
  2. A Valid range is 1-4094. On a VLAN switch, you assign ports with the proper VLAN number.
  3. The switch then allows data which needs to be sent between various ports having the same VLAN.
  4. Since almost all networks are larger than a single switch, there should be a way to send traffic between two switches.
  5. One simple and easy way to do this is to assign a port on each network switch with a VLAN and run a cable between them.

Advantages of VLAN

  1. Here are the important pros/benefits of VLAN:
  2. It solves a broadcast problem.
  3. VLAN allows you to add an additional layer of security.
  4. It allows you to create groups of logically connected devices that act like they are on their own network.
  5. You can logically segment networks based on departments, project teams, or functions.
  6. Higher performance and reduced latency.
  7. Users may work on sensitive information that must not be viewed by other users.
  8. You do not require additional hardware and cabling, which helps you to saves costs
  9. It reduces the number of devices for particular network topology.
  10. VLAN makes managing physical devices less complex.
